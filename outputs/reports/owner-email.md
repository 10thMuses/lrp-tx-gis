Attached is the memo on drilling around Caramba North, written so it can go straight to the buyer. The short version: **the public record supports — clearly — that the buyer's drilling-vibration concern is not borne out at this site.**

**The spine is location, and it is strong.** Counting only genuine *new* wells (completion ≥ spud — recompletion re-stamps excluded): no well of any kind has been spudded within two miles of the tract in over a decade; **no new-drill well within five miles**; only **three new-drill wells within ten miles** across all of 2020–2025 (nearest ≈ 6.9 miles; all three deep ≥9,200 ft, spud 2020 and 2025). Pecos has just ≈116 genuine new wells county-wide since 2020 — about one-tenth of the comparable-county average (≈1,148, across Reeves, Midland, Martin, Reagan, Howard, and Loving) and the least of the seven. The wells within two miles are all decades-old legacy completions (drilled 1950s–2002) — mostly plugged shallow verticals plus a handful of long-completed deep wells.

**Reinforcing that — and this is the key refinement:** the large "shallow" footprint in Pecos is **recompletions, not new drilling.** About 45% of Pecos permits since 2020 are recompletions (rework of existing wellbores — no new hole), and **96% of every recompletion is one operator, Kinder Morgan Production**, reworking existing CO₂-flood fields. Genuine new drilling (~478 permits) is ~97% deep horizontal (Diamondback, XTO, Continental, Gordy) — and, as above, essentially none of it is near the site.

A few things worth your knowing:

- **This is a correction I made after a good catch on the data, and it makes the memo stronger, not weaker.** An earlier draft said "~50% of Pecos drilling is shallow/vertical." On checking the permit `filing_purpose` field, roughly half of that "shallow" activity is **recompletions**, not new wells — so the honest, cleaner statement is that the shallow footprint is rework of existing wells, while genuine new drilling is deep and remote. The proximity conclusion didn't weaken; restricting to new drills it got tighter (zero within five miles).
- **It also resolves an internal tension.** The earlier "~50% shallow" sat awkwardly next to "permits are ~87% horizontal." With recompletions separated out, both reconcile: new drilling is ~97% deep; the shallow signal was rework. Nothing in the memo now contradicts the RRC's own data.
- **Production cross-reference (Finding 9) — re-run on a strict marginal threshold, per your direction.** Two corrections sit underneath the current numbers, both following good catches on the data. **First**, the earlier "none producing within ten miles" was an artifact of a weak wellbore→lease *name* match that only covered ~31% of wells; I rebuilt the join by **API number** through RRC's authoritative API-to-lease crosswalk (≈99.6% coverage). **Second**, the prior 150 bbl/d oil cutoff for "marginal" was too loose — a 100 bbl/d well is not really marginal — so per your direction the threshold is now **gas ≤ 125 Mcf/d AND oil ≤ 25 bbl/d**, a strict marginal/end-of-life rate. Under the corrected join and the strict threshold: within ten miles, **241 of 291 non-plugged new-drill wells (~83%) are marginal or end-of-life**, and the 50 still producing above that threshold are all decades-old completions — about 15 legacy deep-gas wells (1965–1978, ≈17,000–22,800 ft); a cluster of ~31 low-rate (~37.5 bbl/d) vertical conventional oil wells at ≈3,150–3,440 ft (spud 1986–2012, mostly 2007–2012 — a unitized stripper-grade pumping lease); 3 truly shallow (<3,000 ft) oil wells at 2,824–2,945 ft (2004–2011 spud); and the single 2020 deep-horizontal new-drill at 9.37 mi out (Finding 4). Within two miles, 3 of 5 non-plugged are marginal and the 2 still producing are a 1975, ≈22,100-ft gas well at 125.7 Mcf/d and a 2008, ≈3,275-ft vertical oil well at 37.5 bbl/d. County-wide marginal/EoL is ≈12,490 (still producing ≈10,060). **Every still-producing well near the site is a decades-old completion — not an active drilling or hydraulic-fracturing operation** — so the vibration thesis holds: drilling/fracking activity is absent.
