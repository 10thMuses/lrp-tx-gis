[Owner],

Attached is the memo on shallow drilling around Caramba North, written so it can go straight to the buyer. The short version: **the public drilling record strongly supports that the buyer's vibration concern is not borne out at this site.**

What the records show:

- **Nothing has been drilled within two miles of the tract in over a decade** — no well of any kind since before 2015, and no shallow (<3,000 ft) well since 2002. The handful of shallow wells that ever existed near the parcel are 1950s–1990s vintage and are plugged and abandoned.
- **On the tract itself,** the only sub-3,000-ft well was drilled in 1960 and is long since plugged; the one active well is deeper than 3,000 ft and was drilled in 2008.
- **County-wide, almost none of the recent drilling is near the site** — only about 2% of the wells spudded in Pecos since 2020 are within ten miles of the tract; the rest are a median of ~30 miles away, and no drilling permit has been filed within ten miles since 2020.
- **Pecos is a comparatively light-drilling county** — roughly one-third the new drilling of the average peer county since 2020.

A few things I want you to know that I deliberately handled a certain way, so you're not caught off guard if the buyer's people dig in:

- **The argument rests on proximity, not on "the drilling is the harmless kind."** The buyer framed the worry as shallow *vertical* drilling. The Railroad Commission's own permit record shows modern Pecos drilling is actually ~87% *horizontal* (deep). Leaning on a "it's all shallow vertical" story would have been picked apart immediately; instead the memo's spine is that essentially nothing is being drilled at or near the site at all — which is true regardless of well type and is not realistically disputable. The horizontal fact is in the memo, stated plainly, so we're not exposed on it.
- **I had the methods/limitations section removed from the buyer's copy, per your instruction.** For your own awareness: this is a drilling-*record* analysis (where, when, how deep, what orientation) — it is not a geotechnical vibration study. If the buyer pushes for engineering certainty, the clean next step is a short vibration/seismic assessment; the data in this memo would support commissioning one and would underpin its conclusions. I'd recommend we be ready to offer that rather than over-argue the records.
- **One internal data nuance:** the wellbore depth field and the permit orientation field disagree for Pecos. We used the authoritative orientation field and did not put a depth-share statistic in the buyer's copy, because on its face it could be misread against us and it conflicts with the authoritative figure. It's handled — just flagging it exists.
- A full Pecos-vs-peers *orientation* comparison isn't in the memo (the authoritative profile data currently only runs through 2017 for the peer counties); it can be produced with additional work if you ever want it.

Net: I think this is a strong, defensible package to put in front of the buyer, and it's built to hold up if they scrutinize it. Happy to walk through any of it or adjust emphasis before it goes out.
