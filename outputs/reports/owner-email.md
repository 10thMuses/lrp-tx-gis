[Owner],

Attached is the memo on drilling around Caramba North, written so it can go straight to the buyer. The short version: **the public record supports — on two independent grounds — that the buyer's drilling-vibration concern is not borne out at this site.**

**1. The kind of drilling.** The activity that actually produces ground vibration is hydraulic fracturing of deep horizontal wells; shallow vertical conventional wells are not fracked and are not a comparable source. Of the wells actually drilled in Pecos County since 2020, about half are shallow (under 3,000 ft), and — checked well-by-well against the Railroad Commission's own Wellbore Profile field — about **86% of those shallow wells are vertical** (conventional, unfracked). Pecos is far more weighted to shallow vertical drilling than comparable counties: roughly **50% of Pecos wells are shallow vs. ~2% across the peer counties**, whose programs are almost entirely deep horizontal. So Pecos sees comparatively little of the fracking activity at all.

**2. Location — the decisive point.** Whatever the type, drilling is not happening at or near the tract: **no well of any kind spudded within two miles in over a decade**, no shallow well within two miles since 2002, only ~2% of recent county drilling within ten miles, and **no drilling permit filed within ten miles since 2020**. The nearby wells are old, shallow, vertical, and plugged.

A few things worth your knowing:

- **This version is materially stronger than where I started, and it is defensible.** My first cut leaned only on proximity, because the headline RRC permit figure (~87% of *permit applications* horizontal) appeared to cut against a "shallow/vertical" argument. I went back and matched the wells **actually spudded** to the Commission's own orientation field: the shallow ones are ~86% **vertical**. The permits-vs-actually-drilled distinction is the key, and the memo states it plainly, so if the buyer's people raise the 87% permit number we have already addressed it with the agency's own well-level data rather than being rebutted by it.
- **The memo uses RRC's own Wellbore Profile field for the orientation claim** — not a depth assumption — so the central "shallow → vertical → not fracked" point is sourced to the regulator, which is the strongest possible footing.
- **The methods/limitations section is removed from the buyer copy, per your instruction.** For your awareness only: this is a drilling-record analysis, not a geotechnical vibration study. If the buyer wants engineering certainty, the clean next step is a short vibration assessment, which this data would underpin. I'd suggest we be ready to offer that rather than over-argue.
- A full Pecos-vs-peers comparison on the *orientation* field (rather than depth) would require additional data pulls for the peer counties; the depth-based peer comparison in the memo is sound and the Pecos shallow→vertical link is RRC-confirmed.

Net: a strong, two-pillar package — the drilling in Pecos is disproportionately the low-intensity, non-fracked kind, and in any case essentially none of it is near the site. Built to hold up if the buyer scrutinizes it. Happy to walk through it or adjust emphasis before it goes out.
