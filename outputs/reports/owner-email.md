Attached is the memo on drilling around Caramba North, written so it can go straight to the buyer. The short version: **the public record supports — clearly — that the buyer's drilling-vibration concern is not borne out at this site.**

**The spine is location, and it is strong.** Counting only genuine *new* wells (completion ≥ spud — recompletion re-stamps excluded): no well of any kind has been spudded within two miles of the tract in over a decade; **no new-drill well within five miles**; only **three new-drill wells within ten miles** across all of 2020–2025 (nearest ≈ 6.9 miles; all three deep ≥9,200 ft, spud 2020 and 2025). Pecos has just ≈116 genuine new wells county-wide since 2020 — about one-tenth of the comparable-county average (≈1,148; Howard and Loving included, Ward dropped) and the least of the seven. The wells within two miles are all decades-old legacy completions (drilled 1950s–2002) — mostly plugged shallow verticals plus a handful of long-completed deep wells.

**Reinforcing that — and this is the key refinement:** the large "shallow" footprint in Pecos is **recompletions, not new drilling.** About 45% of Pecos permits since 2020 are recompletions (rework of existing wellbores — no new hole), and **96% of every recompletion is one operator, Kinder Morgan Production**, reworking existing CO₂-flood fields. Genuine new drilling (~478 permits) is ~97% deep horizontal (Diamondback, XTO, Continental, Gordy) — and, as above, essentially none of it is near the site.

A few things worth your knowing:

- **This is a correction I made after a good catch on the data, and it makes the memo stronger, not weaker.** An earlier draft said "~50% of Pecos drilling is shallow/vertical." On checking the permit `filing_purpose` field, roughly half of that "shallow" activity is **recompletions**, not new wells — so the honest, cleaner statement is that the shallow footprint is rework of existing wells, while genuine new drilling is deep and remote. The proximity conclusion didn't weaken; restricting to new drills it got tighter (zero within five miles).
- **It also resolves an internal tension.** The earlier "~50% shallow" sat awkwardly next to "permits are ~87% horizontal." With recompletions separated out, both reconcile: new drilling is ~97% deep; the shallow signal was rework. Nothing in the memo now contradicts the RRC's own data.
- **Production cross-reference (Finding 9) — corrected, and now solid.** A second good catch on the data. The earlier draft said "none producing within ten miles," but that rested on a weak wellbore→lease *name* match that only covered ~31% of wells — so "none producing" was really "almost nothing matched." I rebuilt the join by **API number** through RRC's authoritative API-to-lease crosswalk (OG_WELL_COMPLETION_DATA_TABLE), which covers **≈99.6%** of non-plugged wells. The honest, defensible picture: within ten miles **≈95% (275 of 291) of the non-plugged new-drill wells are no longer producing**, but **16 still produce** — and every one is an old (mostly 1965–1978), very deep (≈17,000–22,800 ft) conventional **gas** well at a modest rate (3 within five miles, one ≈1.1 mi out, a 1975/22,100-ft gas well). County-wide the no-longer-producing figure is now ≈13,770 (not the old ~2,030 floor). Net: it still supports the thesis cleanly — no *shallow, new, or hydraulically-fractured* production near the site — but it no longer overclaims "zero production," and because coverage is near-complete I dropped the "floor/imperfect-match" caveat. This is the kind of thing the buyer's technical team would have caught; better that we did. Buyer copy states the corrected figures (without the methods commentary, per your instruction).
- **Methods/limitations remain out of the buyer copy, per your instruction.** For your awareness only: this is a drilling-record analysis, not a geotechnical vibration study. If the buyer wants engineering certainty, a short vibration assessment is the clean next step; this data underpins it.

Net: a location-first package, now built on a clean new-drill-vs-recompletion distinction that holds up under scrutiny. Happy to walk through it or adjust emphasis before it goes out.
