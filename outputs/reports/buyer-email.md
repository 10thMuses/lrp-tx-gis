This transmits our assessment of the oil-and-gas drilling record at and within ten miles of the Caramba North tract, prepared in response to the ground-vibration question raised in connection with the proposed data-center development. The analysis relies exclusively on Railroad Commission of Texas (RRC) primary records — the dbo900 wellbore database and the W-1 drilling-permit record — and is fully reproducible from those sources. The memorandum is attached; the findings are summarized below.

**Proximity.** No well of any kind has been spudded within two miles of the tract in over a decade, and no shallow (<3,000 ft) well within two miles since 2002. Counting only genuine new wells (recompletion re-stamps of existing bores excluded), no new-drill well lies within five miles of the tract, and only three within ten miles across all of 2020–2025 — all deep (≈9,200–9,500 ft, spudded 2020 and 2025), the nearest approximately 6.9 miles out. The wells within two miles are all decades-old legacy completions (drilled 1950s–2002) — mostly plugged shallow verticals plus a handful of long-completed deep wells — none representing active drilling or a hydraulic-fracturing operation. Separately, cross-referencing every well against RRC production records for the most recent six reported months — joined by API number through the RRC's authoritative API-to-lease crosswalk, covering ≈99.6% of non-plugged wells — and applying a strict marginal-well threshold (trailing-average output at or below **125 Mcf/day of gas AND 25 bbl/day of oil**): about **83% of the non-plugged genuine-new-drill wells within ten miles (241 of 291) are marginal or end-of-life**. The 50 still producing above that threshold are all decades-old completions — comprising ~15 legacy deep-gas wells (mostly 1965–1978 spud, ≈17,000–22,800 ft), a cluster of ~31 low-rate vertical conventional oil wells at ≈3,150–3,440 ft on a unitized stripper-grade pumping lease (spud 1986–2012), 3 truly shallow (<3,000 ft) oil wells at 2,824–2,945 ft (spud 2004–2011), and the single 2020 deep-horizontal new-drill at 9.37 mi noted above. A pumping wellhead or low-rate gas well on a decades-old completion is not a drilling rig or a hydraulic-fracturing spread. There is accordingly no active drilling or hydraulic-fracturing operation at or near the site.

**Character of activity.** Filing purpose is taken directly from the RRC permit record, not inferred. Of ≈478 New Drill permits in Pecos since 2020, only ≈116 trace to genuine new wells, and ≈95% of those are deep (≥3,000 ft) horizontal — operator-concentrated (Diamondback, XTO, Continental, Gordy), oil-directed, and remote from the site. Approximately 45% of permits since 2020 are recompletions of existing wellbores rather than new wells, and a single operator — Kinder Morgan Production, the largest filer at ~44% of all permits, reworking existing CO₂-flood fields — accounts for ~96% of those recompletions: enhanced-recovery rework on existing bores, not new drilling. On a genuine-new-drill basis Pecos is by far the least-drilled of the seven comparable Permian counties (≈116 new wells since 2020 vs an average of ≈1,148 across the other six — Reeves, Midland, Martin, Reagan, Howard, Loving). New drilling and the hydraulic-fracturing completions that accompany it — whether of horizontal or vertical wellbores — are the activity materially associated with ground vibration, and that activity is absent from the site's vicinity.

**Conclusion.** The drilling record does not indicate a drilling- or hydraulic-fracturing-related ground-vibration exposure at the Caramba North site.

**Interactive data tool — independent validation.** The complete record is available at **https://lrp-tx-gis.netlify.app**. It carries the full RRC wellbore and W-1 permit records alongside the surrounding energy-infrastructure datasets, and the wells layer is **filtered to genuine new drilling — recompletion re-stamps of existing wellbores are excluded** — so its counts, summary tables, and time series reflect new wellbores, consistent with this memorandum. The capabilities most relevant to this assessment:

- **Summary tables.** A live panel that, for any selection, tabulates new-well counts and annual averages by era, Pecos against the comparable counties, with depth and status breakdowns — the same tabulations that underlie the memorandum.
- **Time series.** A year-by-year scrubber rendering the cumulative new-drill history, so the trend and spatial concentration of activity are directly observable.
- **Reproducible views.** One-click saved views (e.g., Pecos new wells; Pecos vs. comparable counties by depth band) that regenerate each figure in the memorandum, with per-feature attribute detail (API, spud/completion, depth, operator, status, source) and filtering by county, spud year, depth, and wellbore profile.
